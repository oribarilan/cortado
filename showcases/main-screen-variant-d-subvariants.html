Variant D — Single Pane Subvariants

All subvariants use the single-pane expandable pattern: full-width rows, focused item auto-expands detail inline. The design axes explored: detail containment style, row density, and how much of the title is revealed.

Shared behavior: ↑↓ moves focus. Focused item auto-expands its detail below. Enter opens the URL. Esc closes the window. No split pane — the entire window is the list.

Axes explored:
• D1 — Left border accent (like menubar panel's expand)
• D2 — Inset card (stronger visual container for detail)
• D3 — Flush minimal (no decoration, maximum density)
• D4 — Two-line rows + flush expand (richer rows, compact detail)
• D5 — Compact rows + full-title reveal on expand

D1

Left border accent

The expanded detail sits below the row with a teal left border — the same pattern as the menubar panel's expand/collapse. Familiar, clean, and gives a visual anchor for where the detail begins and ends.

D5

Compact rows + full title reveal

Rows stay single-line and compact for fast scanning. When focused, the detail area reveals the full un-truncated title, fields, and open link. The title is shown twice — truncated in the row, full in the expand — solving readability without sacrificing scan density.

My pick: D5 (compact rows + full title reveal) or D1 (left border accent). D5 gives the best of both worlds — maximum row density for scanning with full title readability on focus. D1 is the most consistent with the existing menubar panel pattern. D2 is the most visually "finished" but takes the most vertical space per expanded item.
